Paint Correction FAQ

What is paint correction?

Paint correction is a professional process that removes imperfections from your vehicle’s paint by machine polishing the clear coat. Over time, your paint can develop:

* Swirl marks
* Light scratches
* Oxidation
* Water spots
* Holograms
* Fading or dullness
* Surface contamination

The goal of paint correction is to restore clarity, depth, and gloss while making your paint look significantly cleaner and newer.

Before any correction begins, your vehicle goes through proper preparation which may include:

* Hand wash
* Iron decontamination (removes embedded iron particles)
* Clay bar treatment
* Paint inspection

This ensures the paint is clean and safe to polish.

⸻

What is a 1-Step Paint Correction?

A 1-step correction is ideal for vehicles with light imperfections.

This process includes:

✔ Light cutting polish
✔ Removes minor swirl marks
✔ Improves gloss and shine
✔ Reduces light scratches
✔ Enhances paint clarity

A 1-step correction typically removes 50–70% of paint defects depending on paint condition and hardness.

Best for:

* Newer vehicles
* Light swirl marks
* Vehicles that already have decent paint condition
* Owners wanting a noticeable improvement without heavy correction

⸻

What is a 2-Step Paint Correction?

A 2-step correction is a more aggressive restoration process for vehicles with heavier imperfections.

Step 1: Heavy compound

* Removes deeper scratches
* Corrects oxidation
* Eliminates heavier swirl marks
* Removes deeper paint defects

Step 2: Finishing polish

* Refines the paint
* Restores gloss
* Removes haze from compounding
* Creates a deep mirror-like finish

A 2-step correction can remove 70–90%+ of paint defects, depending on paint thickness and condition.

Best for:

* Older vehicles
* Heavily neglected paint
* Deep swirls and scratches
* Vehicles needing major restoration

Note: Some scratches may be too deep to remove safely if they’ve gone through the clear coat.

⸻

Why does pricing vary?

Every vehicle is different. Pricing depends on:

* Vehicle size
* Paint condition
* Level of contamination
* Time needed for preparation
* Whether your vehicle needs 1-step or 2-step correction

Heavily contaminated vehicles require additional prep time before polishing can safely begin.

Do I need paint protection after correction?

Yes — protection is highly recommended after paint correction.

Once the paint is polished, adding protection helps preserve the results and keeps your vehicle cleaner longer.

Sealant Protection (6–8 months)

Our paint sealant helps protect against:

✔ Dirt buildup
✔ Water spots
✔ UV exposure
✔ Road grime
✔ Minor surface wear

It also adds extra gloss and makes future washes easier.

Can you apply ceramic coating after correction?

Yes. Ceramic coating is a premium option for longer-term protection and durability.

Benefits may include:

* Longer-lasting protection
* Strong water beading
* Easier maintenance
* UV protection
* Enhanced gloss

⸻

How long does paint correction take?

Most paint correction services take anywhere from 1 full day to multiple days, depending on the vehicle size and paint condition.

⸻

Will paint correction remove all scratches?

Not always. Paint correction can remove many imperfections, but scratches that are too deep may require wet sanding, touch-up paint, or may be unsafe to fully remove.
